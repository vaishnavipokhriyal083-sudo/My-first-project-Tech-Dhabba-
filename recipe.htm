BARFI



Ingredients


  • Sugar
  • Ghee
  • Milk
  • Cardamom
  • Full cream milk powder
  • Almonds
  • Cashews
  • Regular milk


Instructions


  1. Prepare the Pan: Line an 8x8 or 9x5 inch baking dish or tray with parchment or butter paper and lightly grease it with a few drops of ghee.
  2. Prepare the Pan: Line an 8x8 or 9x5 inch baking dish or tray with parchment or butter paper and lightly grease it with a few drops of ghee.
  3. Cook the Mixture: Place the pan on the stove over medium-low heat. Stir constantly in a single direction to prevent burning. The sugar will melt first (making the mix thinner), and then it will slowly thicken.
  4. Add Flavorings: About halfway through the cooking process (around the 8-10 minute mark when the mixture begins to thicken), stir in the cardamom powder.
  5. Check for "Dough" Consistency: Continue cooking and stirring until the mixture comes away from the sides and center of the pan, forming a soft, dough-like mass. This usually takes 10 to 12 minutes total. Do not overcook, or the barfi will become too hard or chewy.
  6. Set the Barfi: Immediately turn off the heat and transfer the hot dough mixture into your prepared greased pan. Use a greased spatula to press it down flat and even.

  8. Cool & Cut: Allow the barfi to cool completely at room temperature, then place it in the refrigerator for 1 hour to set. Once firm, lift the parchment paper out, place it on a cutting board, and slice it into squares or diamonds.

Gulab Jamun



Ingredients


  • Sugar
  • Ghee
  • Milk
  • Maida
  • Sooji
  • Baking Powder
  • Cardmom
  • Warm-Milk


Instructions

  1. Make the Sugar Syrup : In a wide pan, combine the sugar and water.Bring to a boil on medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.Add the saffron and cardamom powder.Let it simmer for 4-5 minutes until the syrup is slightly sticky (it should have a 1-string consistency).Turn off the heat and stir in the lemon juice. Cover to keep warm.
  2. Prepare the Dough : In a mixing bowl, whisk together the milk powder, flour, and baking powder.Add the melted ghee and mix with your fingertips.Slowly add warm milk, one tablespoon at a time, to bring the mixture together into a very soft, slightly sticky dough. Do not over-knead it, as this will make the jamuns dense.Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Shape the Balls : Grease your palms lightly with ghee or oil.Pinch small, equal-sized portions of the dough (about the size of a marble, as they will double in size).Roll the dough gently between your palms into smooth, crack-free balls. Smooth surfaces are essential; cracks will cause the balls to break while frying
  4. Fry the Jamuns : Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan/kadai on low to medium-low heat.Test the temperature: drop a tiny piece of dough into the oil. It should rise slowly and gently with small bubbles without changing color immediately.Carefully drop a few balls into the oil. Do not overcrowd the pan.Fry on low heat, continuously moving the oil around the balls (swirling them) so they cook and brown evenly.Once they are a deep, uniform golden-brown, remove them with a slotted spoon and drain the excess oil.
  5. Soak and Serve : Immediately transfer the hot fried balls into the warm (not boiling) sugar syrup.Cover and let them rest in the syrup for at least 1 to 2 hours. They will absorb the syrup and double in size.Serve warm or chilled!

Rasgulla



Ingredients


  • Sugar
  • Vinegar
  • Milk
  • Water
  • Weat Flour
  • Cardmom
  • Warm-Milk


Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chena (Cottage Cheese)
    Boil the Milk: Pour the milk into a wide, heavy-bottomed pan and bring it to a rolling boil over medium heat, stirring
    Curdle: Turn off the heat. Wait about 1-2 minutes, then slowly add the diluted lemon juice or vinegar while stirring continuously. You will see the milk solids separate completely from the greenish-yellow whey.
    Cool and Drain: Pour 1 cup of cold water immediately into the pot. This stops the chena from overcooking and keeps it soft.Strain: Place a strainer lined with a muslin cloth or cheesecloth over a large bowl. Pour the curdled milk into it.
    Rinse and Squeeze: Wash the collected chena under cold running water to remove the sourness of the lemon/vinegar. Bring the edges of the cloth together and squeeze out the excess water.
    Drain: Hang the cloth for about 30 to 45 minutes. The chena should be moist and crumbly, but not overly dry.
  2. Shape the Rasgullas
    Knead: Transfer the chena to a flat plate or board. Using the base of your palm, knead it continuously for 7 to 10 minutes until it becomes completely smooth, soft, and forms a non-sticky dough. (You can mix in the cornstarch/maida at this stage for added binding).
    Roll: Divide the dough into 12 to 15 equal parts. Gently roll them between your palms into perfectly round balls, ensuring there are no cracks.
  3. Cook in Sugar Syrup
    Make the Syrup: In a wide, deep pan (or pressure cooker), add 1 cup of sugar, 4.5 cups of water, and crushed cardamom. Bring it to a rolling boil until the sugar fully dissolves.
    Add Balls: Gently drop the chena balls one by one into the boiling syrup.Cook: Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and let them boil on medium heat for 10 to 12 minutes. The rasgullas will expand to nearly double their original size.
  4. Cool and Serve
    Cooling: Turn off the heat. Let the rasgullas cool completely inside the covered pan. To prevent them from shrinking, you can drop a few cooked rasgullas into a bowl of ice-cold water for 10 minutes before returning them to the syrup.
    Serve: Once cool, the rasgullas are ready to eat. They taste best when chilled in the refrigerator for a few hours before serving.

Bal Mithai



>

Ingredients


  • Sugar
  • Desi Ghee
  • Water
  • khoya
  • Sooji
  • Cardmom Powder
  • Sugar Balls


Instructions


  1. Roast the KhoyaHeat
    the ghee in a heavy-bottomed kadhai (wok) on low heat. Add the grated khoya and stir continuously. Roast it slowly over a steady, low flame for 20 to 30 minutes until it becomes a rich, uniform chocolate-brown color and gives off a nutty aroma.
  2. Make the Fudge Base
    Add 200g of sugar to the browned khoya and mix well. Continue to cook and stir on low heat until the sugar dissolves completely and the mixture thickens into a solid, fudge-like paste that no longer sticks to the sides of the pan.
  3. Cool and Cut
    Grease a flat metal tray or plate with a few drops of ghee. Pour the hot fudge mixture onto the tray, spread it out evenly to about \(\frac{1}{2}\) to \(\frac{3}{4}\)-inch thickness, and let it cool completely. Once hardened and cooled, slice it into rectangular or square blocks.
  4. Coat with Sugar Balls
    In a separate small pan, dissolve the remaining sugar in half a cup of water and boil it briefly to create a sticky, thick sugar syrup. Take each cut piece of fudge, lightly coat it in the syrup (or lightly wet the surfaces), and immediately roll it in a shallow plate full of bal dana (tiny white sugar balls) until every side is fully covered.
  5. Set and Serve
    Let the coated pieces rest and completely set for a few hours. Your Bal Mithai is ready to serve and can be stored in an airtight container

Kalakand



Ingredients


  • Sugar
  • Ghee
  • Full Fat Milk
  • vinegar
  • Cardamom
  • nuts


Instructions


  1. Curdle the Milk: In a heavy-bottomed pan, boil 1.5 liters of milk. Lower the heat, slowly add the lemon juice, and stir until the milk curdles into chenna (soft cottage cheese). Strain out the whey, rinse the chenna with fresh water to remove sourness, and drain completely.
  2. Reduce the Milk: In a separate large kadhai, bring the remaining 1 liter of milk to a boil and let it simmer until it reduces to half its original quantity.
  3. Combine: Add the prepared chenna and sugar to the boiling milk. Cook on medium flame, stirring continuously to prevent sticking.
  4. Caramelize: Keep stirring until the mixture becomes very thick, turns a light golden brown, and begins to leave the sides of the pan. Stir in the cardamom powder and remove from heat.
  5. Set the Mithai: Transfer the hot mixture to a greased tray or tin, press it down flat, and top with chopped nuts
  6. Rest: Cover and let it set for at least 6 to 8 hours (or overnight) so the flavors meld and it gets a rich, grainy texture. Cut into squares and serve!

Ras malai



Ingredients


  • Sugar
  • Ghee
  • Milk
  • Maida
  • Sooji
  • Baking Powder
  • Cardmom
  • Warm-Milk


Instructions


  1. Prepare the Cheese (Chenna)
    Boil 1 liter of milk in a heavy-bottomed pot. Turn off the heat and let it cool for 1-2 minutes.Gradually add the lemon juice-water mixture while stirring continuously until the milk curdles completely (separating the green whey). Strain the mixture through a muslin cloth, rinse with cold water to remove the sourness, and squeeze out the excess water.Tie the cloth and let it hang for 15-20 minutes to drain completely.
  2. Knead and Shape
    Transfer the drained cheese onto a plate. Add 1 tsp of corn flour. Mash and knead the cheese with the base of your palm for 6–8 minutes until it becomes incredibly smooth and pliable.Pinch off tiny, marble-sized portions, roll them perfectly between your palms without cracks, and gently flatten them into discs.
  3. Cook and Soak
    In a wide pan, boil the sugar and water until the sugar dissolves completely.Drop the mini cheese discs into the boiling syrup, cover with a lid, and cook on medium-high heat for 10-12 minutes (they will double in size).Remove them from the syrup and drop them into a bowl of ice-cold water to lock in their spongy shape.
  4. Make the Rabdi and Assemble
    In a separate saucepan, boil the 500 ml of milk for the rabdi. Let it simmer on low until it reduces slightly and thickens.Stir in the sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands. Turn off the heat and let it cool.Gently squeeze the excess water from your cheese discs, place them into the prepared saffron milk, and chill in the refrigerator for at least 3-4 hours before serving.

Laddu



Ingredients


  • Sugar
  • Ghee
  • Besan
  • Flour
  • Almond


Instructions


  1. Make the Batter: Whisk gram flour (besan), water, and a pinch of food coloring (usually orange) into a smooth, thin consistency.
  2. Fry the Boondi: Hold a perforated ladle (jhara) over hot ghee or oil. Pour the batter through the holes to create tiny droplets (boondi) that drop directly into the oil. Fry them until just cooked but not browned (about 30–40 seconds).
  3. Make Sugar Syrup: Heat sugar and water together until the syrup reaches a one-string consistency. Stir in flavorings like cardamom powder and rose essence.
  4. Mix and Shape: Add the drained, fried boondi to the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak until all the syrup is absorbed. Once slightly cooled, grease your hands with ghee and tightly press portions of the mixture into small, perfect spheres.

Kalakand



Ingredients


  • Sugar
  • Ghee
  • Water
  • Cashew
  • Cardamom
  • nuts
  • Silver edible foil


Instructions


  1. Prepare the Cashew Powder
    Grind: Place room-temperature cashews into a dry blender or mixer. Pulse in short intervals until you achieve a fine powder.
    Avoid Over-grinding: Do not run the mixer continuously, or the natural oils will release and turn the cashews into a paste or butter.
    Sift: Sift the cashew powder through a medium-fine sieve. Set aside any large chunks to re-blend later.
  2. Make the Sugar Syrup
    Combine: In a non-stick or heavy-bottomed pan, combine sugar and water. Use a ratio of about ¾ cup of sugar to 90 ml of water for every 1.5 to 2 cups of cashew powder.
    Dissolve: Heat the mixture on low flame until the sugar dissolves completely. There is no need to wait for a 1-thread or 2-thread consistency; as soon as the sugar dissolves, you are ready for the next step.
  3. Cook the Mixture
    Mix: Turn the heat to low and add the cashew powder slowly into the sugar syrup, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.Cook: Keep stirring continuously on a low flame for about 7 to 10 minutes. You can add a splash of rose water or a pinch of cardamom powder at this stage for flavor.
    Form a Dough: Cook until the mixture thickens, starts leaving the sides of the pan, and forms a single, moist dough lump. Remove from heat.
  4. Knead and Roll
    Knead: Transfer the warm dough onto a plate, butter paper, or clean countertop greased lightly with ghee. Add a teaspoon of ghee and knead it gently while it is still warm to make the surface smooth and pliable.
    Roll: Flatten the dough, place a sheet of parchment or butter paper over it, and roll it out with a rolling pin to a thickness of about \(\frac{1}{8}\) to \(\frac{1}{3}\) of an inch (3 to 5 mm).
  5. Cut and Serve
    Garnish (Optional): Gently place edible silver leaf (Chandi ka Vark) over the rolled dough.
    Cut: Let the dough cool completely, then use a sharp knife to cut it into neat diamond shapes.
